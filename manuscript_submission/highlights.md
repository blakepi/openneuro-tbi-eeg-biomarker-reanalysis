---

### Extended highlights (for journals allowing longer bullets)
- A preregistered, leakage-safe reanalysis tested early EEG features as predictors of persistent
  post-concussive symptoms after acute mild TBI in a public OpenNeuro cohort.
- Subject-level folds were frozen and the full statistical hierarchy locked before any
  feature–outcome relationship was examined.
- No resting-state or event-related EEG feature showed stable association with 4-month symptom
  burden; all 95% confidence intervals spanned zero and coefficients were unstable under
  leave-one-out.
- A penalized full-panel model shrank every coefficient to zero, and no model exceeded an
  age-plus-sex baseline out of fold; results are inconclusive and underpowered.
- Feature-identical replication in related public datasets was infeasible because they did not share
  the EEG paradigm and resting state needed to reproduce the locked feature panel.
